Cultural Blog 3: Emotional Intelligence

Blog Entry 3

What learnings did you take from the chapter?

There were several topics discussed. Firstly that emotional intelligence can be divided into 5 main aspects:

  1. Self-awareness
  2. Self-regulation
  3. Motivation
  4. Empathy
  5. Social skills
These aspects can further be grouped as to whether they relate to intrapersonal or interpersonal skills. The first three are intrapersonal, while the last two are interpersonal.

Emotional intelligence is a leading contributer to success professionally. A study mentioned found the top predictors of success in the tech sector were:

  1. Strong achievement drive and high achievement standards
  2. Ability to influence
  3. Conceptual thinking
  4. Analytical ability
  5. Initiative in taking on challenges
  6. Self-confidence
Of that list, 4 related to emotional intelligence and only 2 to intellectual intelligence.

Emotional intelligence, on a personal level, creates the opportunity to actively create a default state of happiness. It also importantly helps to moderate emotions- as one is mindful of their own body, they can be conscious of what emotions are arising and when, meaning it's easier to prevent them from spiralling. The author also mentions that labelling emotions helps to contain them. Learning these skills is possible due to neuroplasticity: that is, the ability of the brain to develop and change through conscious and repeated behaviours and associations.

Training emotional intelligence starts with training attention. Having strong, calm and stable attention provides the base for emotional intelligence, and a calm, rational perspective with which to view emotional states from. It also allows a pause between and emotional trigger and reacting to it, so you can decide whether or not to react at all. The author suggests the best way to train attention is by practising mindful meditation for 2 minutes a day: sitting and simply being, and either focussing on your breathing or on nothing at all (allowing your mind to wander, or not).

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?

I'm actually already fairly familiar with the concept, as mindful meditation is something I've tried before (and something I've learned to use as a tool while dealing with mental health in myself and others- it can help with anxiety when it's not too severe). It's also often how I get to sleep so I guess in that sense I already have a habit of it!
