Cultural Blog, Sprint 4: Daily Mindfulness

24 July, 2018
What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

The problem I have with this exercise is that I haven't seen anyone recently that I think would be open to trying it. That being said, I've been conscious of being mindful when people are listening and giving what they're saying my full attention, which isn't the exercise exactly but incorporates an element of it.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

Primarily the third (one breath a day), but also the second (do less than you can). I think becoming frustrated or bored with it is definitely something that could put me off in the long run if I'm not careful, which the "do less than you can" method avoids. One breath a day also means I can practice it for a short while whenever I remember to, almost regardless of what I'm doing.

Any other musings?

When I was doing the final exercise from the chapter, I noticed I find open meditation much easier to maintain as I don't have to constantly try to rein in distractions and can embrace that so long as I'm mindful of them and pay attention to them fully. I found myself struggling with the focused meditation section- ironically, usually because I was distracted by the thought that I needed to stay focused on breathing!
