Cultural Blog, Sprint 6: Control

30 July, 2018
When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I find it frustrating mostly when something doesn't work the way I expect it to and isn't easily debugged. Mostly this occurs when my logic is wrong, or when the syntax doesn't work in this instance the way I thought it would. The longer I spend debugging stubborn problems, the more frustrated I tend to get.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I found it useful and again, I'm interested to see how I can implement it in life in general. I think it will be quite effective. It was also interesting the sample given of how acceptance of emotions can resolve more serious problems like panic or anxiety, as I have a specific phobia that has improved over the years but still is an ongoing issue. I plan to try this next time it's triggered.
