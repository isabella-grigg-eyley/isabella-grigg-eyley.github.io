Cultural Blog, Sprint 1: Reflection

I think one of the biggest differences I can tell between EDA/DBC and traditional education systems is the flexibility to fit each student, rather than a standardised system that only suits the median student. It's also far more proactive- traditional education systems don't tend to encourage or have personal benefits to giving more and becoming more engaged. So I think this idea of encouraging interactivity and using it as an opportunity for personal growth that has to be earned, rather than given, is positive.

I think engaging with this will be a personal challenge as we are so trained to expect to be given knowledge and skills by ticking boxes and I am no exception to that rule, but I think this is a good opportunity to break that mental habit and develop better skills to self-develop.

I wouldn't say I'm nervous to participate in this kind of learning environment, but I can see that it might be difficult at first to change that kind of expectation mindset, particularly coming from a university environment where it's very much do assignment, be given grade, etc. But I do think I can get a lot from this course both in terms of hard and soft skills that will benefit me in the long run- particularly given development is such a fast-paced and ever-changing field and having the flexibility to keep up with that is so important.
