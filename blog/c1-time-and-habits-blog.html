Blog Entry 2

I think of all the time and habit management techniques, I found the Fogg method the most useful immediately and the most likely to work for me personally. I think attempting to start big behaviours or projects is something I'm guilty of and I'm going to try this out- starting with tiny, achievable changes triggered by things I already do.

That could then lead into getting into the habit of meditating, but realistically I think I'll need to practice the first technique in order to get into a habit of meditation.

The Fogg method is probably going to be useful for me particularly as my ADHD makes getting started and staying on track for certain tasks harder than for neurotypical people, and I think it'll counter those issues well. It also helps with the internal guilt of not immediately being where you want to be (whether that's doing a certain amount of work per day or whatever other habit) and keeping track of my progress with that and slowly incrementing will be very helpful- moreso than attempting to immediately meet those goals then failing and feeling guilty.

I've tried the Pomodoro technique in the past and I've found that it's not ideal for me personally- as mentioned, ADHD can make getting started on tasks difficult, and being interrupted and forced to stop working when I've only just gotten myself engaged with my work tends to make me far less, rather than more productive!

Interestingly I think I've already gotten in the habit of timeboxing without knowing it was a thing- generally I plan to work until a certain time, rather than setting goals based on how much work I get done, and I find it's more productive for me.
